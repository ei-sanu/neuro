General Information About Skin Diseases

Skin diseases are conditions that affect the skin's structure and function, causing changes in appearance, texture, and overall health. They can be categorized into bacterial, viral, fungal, parasitic, autoimmune, allergic, and genetic conditions.

Common Symptoms

  • Itching and redness
  • Swelling and inflammation
  • Bumps and blisters
  • Scaling and dryness
  • Abnormal growths

Common Skin Diseases

Acne

Definition: A skin condition occurring when hair follicles become clogged with oil and dead skin cells.

Symptoms: Pimples, blackheads, whiteheads, cysts

Treatment: Topical treatments, antibiotics, retinoids

Eczema

Definition: An inflammatory skin condition characterized by dry, itchy patches.

Symptoms: Red, inflamed skin, itching, scaling

Treatment: Moisturizing lotions, topical corticosteroids

Psoriasis

Definition: An autoimmune condition causing rapid skin cell growth.

Symptoms: Red patches with silvery scales

Treatment: Topical treatments, phototherapy, biologics

Skin Cancer Types

Melanoma

The most dangerous form of skin cancer, developing from melanocytes.

Warning Signs: ABCDE rule (Asymmetry, Border irregularity, Color variation, Diameter, Evolution)

Basal Cell Carcinoma

The most common type of skin cancer, typically caused by sun exposure.

Squamous Cell Carcinoma

Develops in the squamous cells of the skin's outer layer.

Prevention Tips

  • Use sunscreen daily (SPF 30 or higher)
  • Avoid excessive sun exposure
  • Maintain good skin hygiene
  • Stay hydrated
  • Eat a balanced diet
  • Regular skin checks
